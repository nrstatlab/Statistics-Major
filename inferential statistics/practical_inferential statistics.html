Practical Course — 12 Experiments

Semester III, Course 6 (Practical) — 2 hrs/week, Credit 1. Conclusions to be drawn from p-value and critical value.

List of Practical Experiments (Official Syllabus)

  1. Large sample test for mean and difference of means.
  2. Large sample test for proportion and difference of proportions.
  3. Large sample test for SD and difference of SDs.
  4. Large sample test for correlation coefficient.
  5. Small sample test for mean and difference of means.
  6. Small sample test for correlation coefficient.
  7. Paired t-test (paired samples).
  8. Small sample test for single variance (χ² test) and difference of variances (F test).
  9. χ² test for goodness of fit and independence of attributes.
  10. Non-parametric tests for single sample (run test, sign test, Wilcoxon signed-rank).
  11. Non-parametric tests for related samples (sign test, Wilcoxon signed-rank).
  12. Non-parametric tests for two independent samples (Median, Mann–Whitney U, Wald–Wolfowitz).

Experiment 1 — Large Sample Test for Mean & Difference of Means

PROBLEM 1 (single mean)

From a sample of 100 students, average IQ is 105 with σ = 15. Test \(H_0: \mu = 100\) at 5 %.

\(Z = (105 - 100)/(15/10) = 3.33\). \(|Z| > 1.96\) ⇒ reject \(H_0\). p-value = 2(1−Φ(3.33)) = 0.00087.

PROBLEM 2 (difference)

City A: \(n_1 = 200,\bar X_1 = 28,\sigma_1 = 4\). City B: \(n_2 = 250,\bar X_2 = 26,\sigma_2 = 5\). Test means equal.

\(Z = (28-26)/\sqrt{16/200 + 25/250} = 2/\sqrt{0.18} = 4.71\) ⇒ reject \(H_0\).

Experiment 2 — Test for Proportion(s)

PROBLEM 1

120 of 200 voters favour A. Test \(H_0: p = 0.5\). \(\hat p = 0.6,\; Z = 0.10/\sqrt{0.5 \cdot 0.5/200} = 2.83\) ⇒ reject.

PROBLEM 2

F1: 60/500 defective; F2: 40/400. Pooled \(\hat p = 100/900 = 0.111\). \(Z = (0.12 - 0.10)/\sqrt{0.111 \cdot 0.889 (1/500+1/400)} = 0.02/0.0212 = 0.94\) ⇒ accept \(H_0\).

Experiment 3 — Test for SD(s)

PROBLEM 1

Sample of 100, \(s = 14.2\); test \(H_0: \sigma = 15\). \(Z = (14.2 - 15)/(15/\sqrt{200}) = -0.8/1.061 = -0.754\) ⇒ accept \(H_0\).

PROBLEM 2

\(n_1=144,s_1=8;\; n_2=100,s_2=6\). \(Z = (8-6)/\sqrt{64/288 + 36/200} = 2/\sqrt{0.402} = 3.15\) ⇒ reject \(H_0\).

Experiment 4 — Test for Correlation Coefficient

PROBLEM 1 (\(\rho = 0\))

\(r = 0.6, n = 50\). \(t = 0.6\sqrt{48}/\sqrt{0.64} = 4.16/0.8 = 5.20\) ⇒ reject; correlation significant.

PROBLEM 2 (\(\rho = 0.5\))

\(r = 0.7, n = 28\). Fisher's Z': \(0.5\ln(1.7/0.3) = 0.867\), \(\zeta_0 = 0.5\ln(1.5/0.5) = 0.549\). \(Z = (0.867 - 0.549)\sqrt{25} = 1.59\) ⇒ accept.

Experiment 5 — Small Sample Test for Mean & Difference of Means

PROBLEM 1 (single mean)

Sample of 9, \(\bar X = 47.5,\; s = 4\). Test \(H_0: \mu = 50\). \(t = (47.5-50)/(4/3) = -1.875\). df=8, \(t_{0.025} = 2.306\) ⇒ accept.

PROBLEM 2 (difference)

\(n_1=10,\bar X_1=22,s_1=3;\;n_2=12,\bar X_2=19,s_2=2.5\). \(s_p^2 = (9 \cdot 9 + 11 \cdot 6.25)/20 = 7.49,\; s_p = 2.74\).

\(t = 3/(2.74\sqrt{1/10+1/12}) = 3/1.171 = 2.56\). df=20, \(t_{0.025} = 2.086\) ⇒ reject.

Experiment 6 — Small Sample Test for Correlation Coefficient

PROBLEM

For 18 pairs: \(r = 0.55\). Test \(H_0: \rho = 0\). \(t = 0.55\sqrt{16}/\sqrt{1 - 0.3025} = 2.2/0.835 = 2.635\). df=16, \(t_{0.025} = 2.12\) ⇒ reject.

Experiment 7 — Paired t-test

PROBLEM

Heart-rate before/after a workout for 12 patients gives differences \(d\): 5,7,4,6,8,3,5,7,6,4,5,6.

\(\bar d = 5.5,\; s_d \approx 1.50\). \(t = 5.5/(1.50/\sqrt{12}) = 5.5/0.433 = 12.7\). df = 11, highly significant.

Experiment 8 — χ² for Variance & F-test for Equality of Variances

PROBLEM 1 (χ²)

\(n=20, s^2=12;\) test \(H_0: \sigma^2 = 9\). \(\chi^2 = 19 \cdot 12/9 = 25.33\). df=19; critical (two-tailed at 5 %): 8.91 and 32.85 ⇒ accept.

PROBLEM 2 (F-test)

\(n_1=21,s_1^2=20;\; n_2=16,s_2^2=8\). \(F = 20/8 = 2.5\). df=(20,15), \(F_{0.05}=2.33\) ⇒ reject \(H_0\); variances differ.

Experiment 9 — χ² Goodness of Fit & Independence

GOODNESS OF FIT

Among 100 patients on a drug, recoveries by week: 22, 27, 25, 26 (expected 25 each).

\(\chi^2 = (9+4+0+1)/25 = 0.56\). df=3, \(\chi^2_{0.05}=7.81\) ⇒ accept (uniform recovery rate).

INDEPENDENCE

Smoking × Cancer 2×2 with O = (60, 40, 20, 80). Expected = (40, 60, 40, 60). \(\chi^2 = 33.33\). df=1 ⇒ reject (smoking ≠ independent of cancer).

Experiment 10 — Non-parametric Tests for One Sample

RUNS TEST

Series of signs +/− for 12 observations of stock returns: + + − − − + + + − − + +. n₁=7, n₂=5, R=5. \(E(R)=2 \cdot 7 \cdot 5/12 + 1 = 6.83;\; \text{Var}(R) = 2 \cdot 7 \cdot 5(70-12)/(144 \cdot 11) = 2.56;\; Z = (5-6.83)/1.6 = -1.14\) ⇒ accept (random).

SIGN TEST + WILCOXON

Test median = 50 with sample 45, 48, 52, 47, 55, 49, 53, 50, 51, 46.

Drop the 50: 9 obs left. Signs: − − + − + − + + −, with 4 plus and 5 minus. P(B(9, 0.5) ≤ 4) = 0.5 ⇒ accept.

Wilcoxon: differences −5, −2, +2, −3, +5, −1, +3, +1, −4. |d|: 5,2,2,3,5,1,3,1,4 ranked (with ties averaged).

Experiment 11 — Non-parametric Tests for Related Samples

SIGN TEST

For 10 paired samples, 7 had positive differences, 3 negative. n=10, S=7. P(B(10, 0.5) ≥ 7) = 0.172, two-tailed p = 0.344 ⇒ accept H₀.

WILCOXON SIGNED-RANK

For paired data with \(W^+ = 48, W^- = 7\). \(W = 7\) for n=10. Critical Wilcoxon = 8 at 5 % (two-tailed); \(W < 8\) ⇒ reject H₀.

Experiment 12 — Non-parametric Tests for Two Independent Samples

MEDIAN TEST

2×2 table for two groups with 4/6 and 8/2 above the grand median. Expected = 6 each. \(\chi^2 = (2)^2/6 + ... = 5.33\). df=1, \(\chi^2_{0.05}=3.84\) ⇒ reject; medians differ.

MANN–WHITNEY U

n₁=5, n₂=6 with R₁=20. \(U_1 = 20 - 15 = 5,\; U_2 = 25\). \(U = 5\). Critical at 5 % two-tailed = 3 ⇒ accept H₀.

WALD–WOLFOWITZ

Combined ranked sequence ABBABABA (n₁=4,n₂=4). R=7. \(E(R) = 2 \cdot 4 \cdot 4/8 + 1 = 5,\; \text{Var}(R) \approx 1.71,\; Z = 1.53\) ⇒ accept H₀.

Lab Notebook Format

  1. Experiment number and title
  2. Aim and hypotheses (H₀, H₁)
  3. Test statistic chosen and its distribution
  4. Computation
  5. Conclusion drawn from both p-value and critical value (per syllabus note)
Important: The syllabus instructs students to draw conclusions of practical problems based on both p-value and critical value approaches. Always state both.
