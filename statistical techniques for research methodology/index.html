Statistical Techniques for Research Methodology

B.Sc. Statistics (Honours) — Semester VI, Course 15 A

Welcome to Course 15 A

This is the complete study package for Statistical Techniques for Research Methodology — five modules that walk you through the entire research pipeline: foundations of research, survey methodology and data collection, processing and analysis of data, questionnaire design and fieldwork, and report writing and presentation. Each concept has two worked examples.

Pre-requisite: Course 5 (Statistical Methods) for correlation, regression, and chi-square. Course 6 (Inferential Statistics) for estimation and hypothesis testing. Course 7 (Sampling Techniques) for sample design, stratification, and cluster sampling.

Course Outcomes

  1. Understand the meaning, objectives, and types of research.
  2. Familiarise with real-time data collection through surveys and sampling designs.
  3. Process and analyse data using appropriate statistical techniques.
  4. Design questionnaires, collect survey data, and draw valid inferences.
  5. Prepare and present research reports in a professional format.

Units in this Course

UNIT 1

Introduction to Research

Meaning, objectives and motivation in research; types of research; research approach; significance of research; research problems — definition, selection and necessity.

 UNIT 2

Survey Methodology and Data Collection

Inference and error in surveys; target populations; sampling frames and coverage error; methods of data collection; non-response; questions and answers in surveys.

 UNIT 3

Processing, Data Analysis and Interpretation

Review of statistical techniques for data analysis; techniques of interpretation; precautions in interpretation.

 UNIT 4

Questionnaire Design and Fieldwork

Develop a questionnaire; collect survey data on a research problem; interpret results and draw inferences.

 UNIT 5

Report Writing and Presentation

Formats of reports; parts of a report; typing and proof-reading; presentation packages; audio-visual aids; presenter's poise.

 PRACTICAL

Project Work

Submit a project report based on an empirical study on a real-life situation — from questionnaire design through analysis to final report.

 REFERENCE
