Domain Elective Tracking Regulation

University Registration Rules: In the III Year (Semesters V & VI), students are required to select a paired set of domain electives. If set 'A' is chosen, the student must complete 12 A (Operations Research), 13 A (Optimization Techniques), 14 A (Statistical Quality Control), and 15 A (Statistical Techniques for Research Methodology) to ensure specialization depth across both semesters.

Detailed Structural Units

Unit 1: Introduction to Research

Introduction: Meaning, objection and motivation in research, types of research, research approach, significance of research. Research problems: definition, selection and necessity of research problems.

Unit 2: Survey Methodology and Data Collection

Survey Methodology and Data Collection, inference and error in surveys, the target populations, sampling frames and coverage error, methods of data collection, non-response, questions and answers in surveys.

Unit 3: Processing, Data Analysis and Interpretation

Processing, Data Analysis and Interpretation: Review of various techniques for data analysis covered in core statistics papers, techniques of interpretation, precaution in interpretation.

Unit 4: Questionnaire Design and Fieldwork

Develop a questionnaire, collect survey data pertaining to a research problem (such as gender discriminations in private v/s government sector, unemployment rates, removal of subsidy, impact on service class v/s unorganized sectors), interpret the results and draw inferences.

Unit 5: Report Writing and Presentation

Formats of Reports: introduction, parts of a report, cover and title page, introductory pages, text, reference section, typing instructions, copy reading, proof reading. Presentation of a report: introduction, communication dimensions, presentation package, audio-visual aids, presenter's poise.
