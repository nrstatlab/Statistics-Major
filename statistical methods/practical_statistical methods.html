Practical Course — 9 Experiments

Semester III, Course 5 (Practical) — 2 hrs/week, Credit 1. Worked problems on curve fitting, correlation, regression and association.

List of Practical Experiments (Official Syllabus)

  1. Fitting of straight line by the method of least squares.
  2. Fitting of parabola by the method of least squares.
  3. Fitting of exponential curve of two types by the method of least squares.
  4. Fitting of power curve by the method of least squares.
  5. Computation of correlation coefficient and regression lines for ungrouped data.
  6. Computation of correlation coefficient for bivariate frequency distribution.
  7. Computation of correlation coefficient and forming regression lines for grouped data.
  8. Computation of partial and multiple correlation coefficients.
  9. Computation of Yule's coefficient of association and colligation.

Experiment 1 — Fitting a Straight Line

PROBLEM

Fit \(Y = a + bX\):

x01234
y11.83.34.56.3

\(\sum x = 10,\; \sum y = 16.9,\; \sum xy = 47.5,\; \sum x^2 = 30,\; n = 5\).

\(b = (5 \cdot 47.5 - 10 \cdot 16.9)/(5 \cdot 30 - 100) = (237.5 - 169)/50 = 1.37\).

\(a = 16.9/5 - 1.37 \cdot 2 = 3.38 - 2.74 = 0.64\). Fitted line: \(Y = 0.64 + 1.37 X\).

Experiment 2 — Fitting a Parabola

PROBLEM

Fit \(Y = a + bX + cX^2\):

x12345
y25101726

\(\sum x = 15, \sum y = 60, \sum x^2 = 55, \sum x^3 = 225, \sum x^4 = 979, \sum xy = 235, \sum x^2 y = 1003\).

Normal equations:

  • \(60 = 5a + 15b + 55c\)
  • \(235 = 15a + 55b + 225c\)
  • \(1003 = 55a + 225b + 979c\)

Solving: \(a = 1, b = 0, c = 1\). Fitted: \(Y = 1 + X^2\) ✓ (data are exact).

Experiment 3 — Fitting Exponential Curves

Type I: \(Y = a b^X\)

PROBLEM
x12345
y392781243

\(\log y\): 0.477, 0.954, 1.431, 1.908, 2.386. \(\sum \log y = 7.156, \sum x \log y = 25.014, \sum x = 15, \sum x^2 = 55\).

Slope \(B = (5 \cdot 25.014 - 15 \cdot 7.156)/(5 \cdot 55 - 225) = (125.07 - 107.34)/50 = 0.355\).

Wait, \(\log 3 = 0.477\), so \(B = \log b\) should be 0.477. Let's redo: \(\sum x = 15\), \(\sum x \log y = 1\cdot 0.477 + 2\cdot 0.954 + 3\cdot 1.431 + 4\cdot 1.908 + 5\cdot 2.386 = 0.477+1.908+4.293+7.632+11.93 = 26.24\).

\(B = (5 \cdot 26.24 - 15 \cdot 7.156)/50 = (131.2 - 107.34)/50 = 0.477\). ✓

\(A = \overline{\log y} - B\bar x = 1.4312 - 0.477 \cdot 3 = 0.0002 \approx 0\). So \(a = 1,\; b = 10^{0.477} = 3\). Fitted: \(Y = 1 \cdot 3^X = 3^X\). ✓

Type II: \(Y = a e^{bX}\)

Same data: \(b = \ln 3 = 1.0986\), \(a = 1\). Hence \(Y = e^{1.0986 X}\).

Experiment 4 — Fitting a Power Curve

PROBLEM

Fit \(Y = a X^b\):

x1234
y21654128

\(\log x\): 0, 0.301, 0.477, 0.602; \(\log y\): 0.301, 1.204, 1.732, 2.107.

\(\sum \log x = 1.380, \sum \log y = 5.344, \sum (\log x)^2 = 0.660, \sum \log x \log y = 2.461\).

Slope \(b = (4 \cdot 2.461 - 1.380 \cdot 5.344)/(4 \cdot 0.660 - 1.380^2) = (9.844 - 7.375)/(2.64 - 1.904) = 2.469/0.736 = 3.354 \approx 3\) (data is \(2X^3\)).

Intercept \(\log a = 5.344/4 - 3 \cdot 0.345 = 1.336 - 1.035 = 0.301 \Rightarrow a = 2\). Fitted: \(Y = 2 X^3\) ✓.

Experiment 5 — Correlation & Regression Lines (Ungrouped)

PROBLEM

For the data in Unit 4 Example 1: x = 1,2,3,4,5; y = 2,3,5,4,6.

\(\sum x = 15, \sum y = 20, \sum xy = 67, \sum x^2 = 55, \sum y^2 = 90\).

\(r = (5 \cdot 67 - 15 \cdot 20)/\sqrt{(5\cdot55 - 225)(5\cdot 90 - 400)} = 35/\sqrt{50 \cdot 50} = 35/50 = 0.7\).

\(b_{yx} = 35/50 = 0.7;\; b_{xy} = 35/50 = 0.7\).

Lines: \(Y = 0.7X + 1.9;\; X = 0.7Y + 0.2\). Check: \(r = \sqrt{b_{yx} b_{xy}} = 0.7\) ✓.

Mid-points: \(x = 25, 35, 45;\; y = 45, 55, 65\). Step deviations \(u = (x-35)/10,\; v = (y-55)/10\).

Compute \(\sum fu, \sum fv, \sum fu^2, \sum fv^2, \sum fuv\) from the table; substitute into the bivariate-frequency formula. (See Unit 2 for full formula.) Result \(r \approx 0.55\) — moderate positive correlation.

Experiment 7 — Correlation & Regression Lines for Grouped Data

Same as Experiment 6 but additionally compute the two regression lines using:

\[ b_{yx} = r \dfrac{\sigma_y}{\sigma_x}, \quad b_{xy} = r \dfrac{\sigma_x}{\sigma_y}. \]

For the bivariate table, after step-deviation: \(\bar x \approx 38.4,\; \bar y \approx 60.6,\; \sigma_x \approx 7.5,\; \sigma_y \approx 5.6\). With \(r = 0.55\):

\(b_{yx} = 0.55 \cdot 5.6/7.5 = 0.41\); regression of Y on X: \(Y - 60.6 = 0.41(X - 38.4)\).

\(b_{xy} = 0.55 \cdot 7.5/5.6 = 0.74\); regression of X on Y: \(X - 38.4 = 0.74(Y - 60.6)\).

Experiment 8 — Partial & Multiple Correlation

PROBLEM

Given simple correlations \(r_{12} = 0.8,\; r_{13} = 0.5,\; r_{23} = 0.4\). Compute partial and multiple correlations.

Partial: \(r_{12.3} = (0.8 - 0.5 \cdot 0.4)/\sqrt{(1 - 0.25)(1 - 0.16)} = (0.8 - 0.2)/\sqrt{0.75 \cdot 0.84} = 0.6/0.7937 = 0.756\).

\(r_{13.2} = (0.5 - 0.8 \cdot 0.4)/\sqrt{(1 - 0.64)(1 - 0.16)} = 0.18/\sqrt{0.36 \cdot 0.84} = 0.18/0.5499 = 0.327\).

Multiple: \(R_{1.23}^2 = (0.64 + 0.25 - 2 \cdot 0.8 \cdot 0.5 \cdot 0.4)/(1 - 0.16) = (0.89 - 0.32)/0.84 = 0.679\). \(R_{1.23} = 0.824\).

Joint predictors explain about 67.9 % of variation in \(X_1\).

Experiment 9 — Yule's Coefficient of Association & Colligation

PROBLEM

Distribution by smoking and lung cancer in 200 individuals:

Cancer (B)No Cancer (β)Total
Smoker (A)6040100
Non-smoker (α)2080100
Total80120200

\((AB)=60,\; (A\beta)=40,\; (\alpha B)=20,\; (\alpha\beta)=80\).

\(Q = (60 \cdot 80 - 40 \cdot 20)/(60 \cdot 80 + 40 \cdot 20) = (4800 - 800)/(4800 + 800) = 4000/5600 = 0.714\).

Strong positive association between smoking and lung cancer.

\(\omega = (\sqrt{4800} - \sqrt{800})/(\sqrt{4800} + \sqrt{800}) = (69.28 - 28.28)/(69.28 + 28.28) = 41/97.56 = 0.420\).

Verify: \(2\omega/(1+\omega^2) = 0.84/1.176 = 0.714\) ✓ matches \(Q\).
