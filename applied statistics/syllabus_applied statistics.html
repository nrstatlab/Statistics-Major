Course 9 — Applied Statistics

Course Information

Year / SemesterII / Semester IV
Course Number9
TitleApplied Statistics
Theory Credits3 (3 hrs/week)
Practical Credits1 (2 hrs/week)

Course Outcomes

  1. Master forecasting techniques and their applications.
  2. Interpret and use a range of index numbers commonly used in business.
  3. Perform calculations involving simple and weighted index numbers.
  4. Understand the basic structure of the Consumer Price Index and perform calculations involving its use.
  5. Apply various data-collection methods for better policy insight.
  6. Construct and implement life tables.
  7. Use population growth curves, estimates and projections.
  8. Implement demographic concepts on real data through practicals.

Theory — Five Units

Unit 1: Time Series

Time series and its components with illustrations; additive, multiplicative and mixed models. Trend — estimation by free-hand curve method, semi-averages, least squares (linear and parabolic), moving averages method.

Open Unit 1 →

Unit 2: Seasonal Component

Determination of seasonal indices by Simple Averages method, Ratio to Moving Average, Ratio to Trend and Link Relatives methods; deseasonalisation.

Open Unit 2 →

Unit 3: Index Numbers

Concept, construction, problems, uses and limitations of index numbers. Simple and weighted index numbers — various weighted aggregate index numbers; criteria of a good index number; Fisher's ideal index. Cost of living index and wholesale price index.

Open Unit 3 →

Unit 4: Vital Statistics

Introduction, definition and uses of vital statistics; sources of vital statistics. Measures of mortality rates — Crude Death Rate, Specific Death Rate, Standardised Death Rate with different populations and problems.

Open Unit 4 →

Unit 5: Life Tables & Fertility

Life table — columns, construction and uses of life table; proofs of life-table functions. Measures of fertility rates — Crude Birth Rate, General Fertility Rate, Age-Specific Fertility Rate, Total Fertility Rate. Measures of population growth — Pearl's vital index, Gross Reproduction Rate, Net Reproduction Rate and related problems.

Open Unit 5 →

Practical — List of Experiments (13)

  1. Measurement of trend by moving averages (odd and even period).
  2. Measurement of trend by least squares (linear and parabolic).
  3. Determination of seasonal indices by method of simple averages.
  4. Determination of seasonal indices by Ratio to Moving Averages.
  5. Determination of seasonal indices by Ratio to Trend.
  6. Determination of seasonal indices by Link Relatives.
  7. Computation of simple index numbers.
  8. Computation of all weighted index numbers.
  9. Computation of reversal tests.
  10. Computation of various mortality rates.
  11. Computation of various fertility rates.
  12. Computation of various reproduction rates.
  13. Construction of a life table.
