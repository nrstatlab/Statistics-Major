Practical Course — 13 Experiments

Semester IV, Course 9 (Practical) — 2 hrs/week, Credit 1. Worked problems on trend, seasonal indices, index numbers, vital statistics and reproduction rates.

List of Practical Experiments (Official Syllabus)

  1. Measurement of trend by method of moving averages (odd and even period).
  2. Measurement of trend by method of Least squares (linear and parabola).
  3. Determination of seasonal indices by method of simple averages.
  4. Determination of seasonal indices by method of Ratio to Moving Averages.
  5. Determination of seasonal indices by method of Ratio to Trend.
  6. Determination of seasonal indices by method of Link Relatives.
  7. Computation of simple index numbers.
  8. Computation of all weighted index numbers.
  9. Computation of reversal tests.
  10. Computation of various Mortality rates.
  11. Computation of various Fertility rates.
  12. Computation of various Reproduction rates.
  13. Construction of Life Table.

Experiment 1 — Trend by Moving Averages

PROBLEM (3-yearly MA)

Production (lakh tonnes): 10, 12, 14, 16, 20, 22, 24, 26.

3-yearly MA: 12.00, 14.00, 16.67, 19.33, 22.00, 24.00 (placed at year 2 to year 7).

PROBLEM (4-yearly centred MA)

Data: 5, 7, 11, 17, 25, 31, 40, 50.

4-yr MAs (placed between years): 10, 15, 21, 28.25, 33.25, 36.5.

Centred MAs at years 3, 4, 5, 6: (10+15)/2 = 12.5; 18; 24.625; 30.75.

Experiment 2 — Trend by Least Squares

LINEAR (8-year data)

Years: 2018–2025 with sales 10, 12, 14, 16, 20, 22, 24, 26. Code X = −7,−5,−3,−1,1,3,5,7 (half-year units).

\(\sum Y = 144,\; \sum X^2 = 168,\; \sum XY = 322\). \(a = 18\); \(b = 322/168 = 1.917\).

Trend (per half-year unit): \(\hat Y = 18 + 1.917 X\); per year: slope ≈ 3.83.

PARABOLA (5-year data)

Production: 5, 7, 11, 17, 25 with X = −2,−1,0,1,2.

\(\sum Y = 65,\;\sum X^2 = 10,\;\sum X^4 = 34,\;\sum XY = 38,\;\sum X^2 Y = 156\).

Solve: \(a = 9.286,\; b = 3.8,\; c = 1.857\). Trend: \(\hat Y = 9.286 + 3.8 X + 1.857 X^2\).

Using the above data, compute 4-quarter centred MAs (8 values from 12). Then form ratios Y/MA × 100 for each available period; average across years by quarter; correct so sum = 400.

Experiment 5 — Seasonal Indices by Ratio to Trend

Fit linear trend \(T_t = 50 + 2t\) for the same data. Compute ratios (actual/trend × 100). Average ratios per quarter. Adjust so sum = 400.

Illustrative ratios in 2023 Q1–Q4: 115.4, 148.1, 128.6, 117.2. Across 3 years, the seasonal pattern emerges after averaging.

Experiment 6 — Seasonal Indices by Link Relatives

Compute period-to-period ratios \(Y_t/Y_{t-1} \times 100\). Average across years by quarter. Build chain relatives starting at 100; correct so the chain returns to 100 after one full cycle. Adjust to sum 400.

Experiment 9 — Reversal Tests

Time Reversal Test (TRT) for Fisher:

\(P_{01}^F \times P_{10}^F = \sqrt{\dfrac{\sum p_1 q_0}{\sum p_0 q_0} \cdot \dfrac{\sum p_1 q_1}{\sum p_0 q_1}} \cdot \sqrt{\dfrac{\sum p_0 q_1}{\sum p_1 q_1} \cdot \dfrac{\sum p_0 q_0}{\sum p_1 q_0}} = 1\) ✓.

Factor Reversal Test (FRT) for Fisher:

\(P_{01}^F \times Q_{01}^F = \sqrt{\dfrac{\sum p_1 q_0 \cdot \sum p_1 q_1}{\sum p_0 q_0 \cdot \sum p_0 q_1}} \cdot \sqrt{\dfrac{\sum p_0 q_1 \cdot \sum p_1 q_1}{\sum p_0 q_0 \cdot \sum p_1 q_0}} = \dfrac{\sum p_1 q_1}{\sum p_0 q_0}\) ✓ — equals the value index.

Verify numerically: \(P_F = 1.4614\), \(Q_F = \sqrt{(158/165)(232/240)} = \sqrt{0.9576 \times 0.9667} = 0.9621\). Product = 1.4614 × 0.9621 = 1.406, and \(V = 232/165 = 1.406\) ✓.

Experiment 11 — Fertility Rates

DATA

Population 1 000 000; births 18 000. Female population 15–49 = 250 000.

ASFRs (per 1 000): 40, 120, 140, 80, 40, 10, 2 for age groups 15–19, 20–24, ..., 45–49.

CBR = 18 per 1 000. GFR = 18 000/250 000 × 1 000 = 72 per 1 000 women.

TFR = (40 + 120 + 140 + 80 + 40 + 10 + 2) × 5/1 000 = 432 × 5/1 000 = 2.16 children per woman.

Experiment 12 — Reproduction Rates

From Experiment 11: TFR = 2.16; female-birth fraction = 0.487.

  • GRR = 2.16 × 0.487 = 1.052 daughters per woman.
  • NRR (assume mean survival probability 0.92): NRR = 1.052 × 0.92 = 0.968.
  • NRR < 1 ⇒ population declining over the long run.
  • Pearl's Vital Index = B/D × 100 = 18 000/8 000 × 100 = 225.
