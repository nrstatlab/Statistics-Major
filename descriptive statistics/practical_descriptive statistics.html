Practical Course — 10 Experiments

Semester I, Course 1 (Practical) — 2 hrs/week, Credit 1. Worked problem with data & solution for each experiment.

List of Practical Experiments (Official Syllabus)

  1. Writing a Questionnaire in different situations.
  2. Forming a grouped and ungrouped frequency distribution table.
  3. Diagrammatic presentation of data — Bar, multiple Bar and Pie.
  4. Graphical presentation of data — Histogram, frequency polygon, Ogives.
  5. Computation of measures of central tendency — Mean, Median and Mode.
  6. Computation of measures of dispersion — QD, MD and SD.
  7. Computation of non-central, central moments, β₁ and β₂ for ungrouped data.
  8. Computation of non-central, central moments, β₁, β₂ and Sheppard's corrections for grouped data.
  9. Computation of Karl Pearson's and Bowley's coefficients of skewness.
  10. Computation of kurtosis.

Experiment 1 — Writing a Questionnaire

AIM

To frame a structured questionnaire for a given research situation.

Guidelines for a Good Questionnaire

  1. Cover letter explaining purpose and confidentiality.
  2. Questions should be short, simple, unambiguous.
  3. Avoid leading and double-barrelled questions.
  4. Use closed (multiple-choice) questions where possible.
  5. Logical flow — easy to difficult, general to specific.
  6. Pilot-test before full deployment.
SITUATION 1

Survey on Reading Habits of College Students

  1. Name (optional): ____________________
  2. Age: __ Gender: ☐ Male ☐ Female ☐ Other
  3. Class / Year of study: __________
  4. How often do you read non-academic books? ☐ Daily ☐ Weekly ☐ Monthly ☐ Rarely ☐ Never
  5. Preferred genre: ☐ Fiction ☐ Biography ☐ Science ☐ Self-help ☐ Other ____
  6. Average hours per week spent reading: ☐ <2 ☐ 2–5 ☐ 5–10 ☐ >10
  7. Source of books: ☐ Library ☐ Bookstore ☐ Online ☐ E-books
  8. Suggestions to encourage reading in college: ____________
SITUATION 2

Customer Satisfaction Survey for a Mobile Service Provider

  1. How long have you used our service? ☐ <6 m ☐ 6 m–1 y ☐ 1–3 y ☐ >3 y
  2. Rate call quality: 1 (Poor) — 5 (Excellent)
  3. Rate data speed: 1 — 5
  4. Rate customer support: 1 — 5
  5. Did you face any billing issue in last 3 months? Yes / No
  6. Likely to recommend us to a friend (NPS): 0 — 10
  7. Suggestions for improvement: ______________

About \(A = 25\): \(\mu'_1 = 0,\; \mu'_2 = 120,\; \mu'_3 = 0,\; \mu'_4 = 36 000\).

Since \(\mu'_1 = 0\), the central moments equal the raw moments here: \(\mu_2 = 120,\; \mu_3 = 0,\; \mu_4 = 36 000\).

Sheppard's correction with \(h = 10\):

  • \(\mu_2^* = 120 - 100/12 = 111.67\).
  • \(\mu_3^* = 0\) (no correction).
  • \(\mu_4^* = 36 000 - (100/2)(120) + 7(10000)/240 = 36 000 - 6 000 + 291.67 = 30 291.67\).

\(\beta_1 = 0\); \(\beta_2 = 30 291.67 / 111.67^2 = 30 291.67 / 12 470.18 \approx 2.43\) (platykurtic).

Experiment 9 — Karl Pearson & Bowley Skewness

KARL PEARSON

From Experiment 5: Mean = 31.25, Mode = 33.33, SD = 12.69.
\(S_k = (31.25 - 33.33)/12.69 = -0.164\) (slightly negatively skewed).
Or via empirical relation: \(S_k = 3(31.25 - 32)/12.69 = -0.177\). Both agree.

BOWLEY

From Experiment 6: \(Q_1 = 22.5,\; Q_2 = \) Median \(= 32,\; Q_3 = 40\).
\(S_B = (40 + 22.5 - 64)/(40 - 22.5) = -1.5/17.5 = -0.086\) (very mildly negative).

Experiment 10 — Kurtosis

PROBLEM

Use Experiment 7 results: \(\mu_2 = 8,\; \mu_4 = 108.8\).
\(\beta_2 = 108.8 / 64 = 1.70\). Since \(\beta_2 < 3\), distribution is Platykurtic.
\(\gamma_2 = \beta_2 - 3 = -1.30\).

SECOND PROBLEM

Suppose \(\mu_2 = 4\) and \(\mu_4 = 75\). \(\beta_2 = 75/16 = 4.6875\) → Leptokurtic.
\(\gamma_2 = 1.6875\).
